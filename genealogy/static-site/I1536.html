The Salem News May 30, 1940

MRS. LUCINDA ANN

ROUDEBUCH SERVICES

Friends and relatives paid their last respects to Mrs. Lucinda AnnRoudebuch Monday at services held in the Salem Friends church at 2:00p.m. Services were conducted by Mrs. Bessie F. Collins, taking her textfrom 1 Pet. 1:17. Rev. Paul W .Barnett read the scripture and offeredprayer. The songs "Looking This Way" and "Beautiful Eden" were sungbyRev. and Mr. Barnett accompanied by Mrs. Clarence Peterson. Intermentwas in East cemetery; pallbearers were Victor Morrow, V. L. Cahoon,H. A.Kester, Oliver Goldsmith, Jess friend and Charles Tyner.

Lucinda Ann Roudebuch, daughter of Benjamin and Matilda Long, was bornnear Salem on July 24, 1861 and died May 25, 1940 aged 78 years and 10months. The greater part of her life was lived in Henry county and thelast years in Salem. After her marriage to Fred Roudebuch she resided inFort Madison for some years. She became a member of a church and wasactive in its work. Later she returned to Salem to care for her agedparents who wit ha brother, John Preceded her in death.

Left to mourn her are a sister-in-law, Ellen Long and nieces andnephews. Mrs. Roudebuch's passing will leave an irreparable loss inthiscommunity.
