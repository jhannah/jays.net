Burlington Hawk-eye

Sunday, April 23, 2000

Arlo Humphrey

Arlo E. Humphrey, 85, of Farmington, died at 4 a.m. Saturday, April 22,2000, at Van Buren County Memorial Hospital in Keosauqua.

Born Dec. 31, 1914, in Jefferson County, he was the son of Bert andMaggie Hudson Humphrey. On July 17, 1954, he married Frances Davis Hedgerin Farmington.

Mr. Humphrey was a farmer and later worked for Sieg Auto Parts storeinFort Madison. He was a 46-year resident of Farmington.

He was a member of American Legion Post 94 of Salem and FarmingtonMethodist Church. He was a World War II Army veteran, receiving a PurpleHeart.

Survivors include his wife; one son, Leroy Hedger of Panama City, Fla.;one daughter, Mary Menke of West Point; four grandchildren; fourgreat-grandchildren; and two brothers, Earl Humphrey of Mount Pleasantand Howard Humphrey of Burlington.

He was preceded in death by his parents, one grandson, two brothers andone sister.

Visitation will begin after 1 p.m. Monday at Hofbauer Funeral Home inFarmington, where the family will receive friends from 7 to 8 p.m.

The funeral for Mr. Humphrey will be at 11 a.m. Tuesday at HofbauerFuneral Home, with the Revs. Bill Jenkins and Robert Hambrightofficiating. Burial will be in Greenglade Cemetery in Farmington.

Memorials have been established for Farmington Methodist Church and theFarmington EMTs.
